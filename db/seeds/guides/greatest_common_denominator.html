The greatest common denominator (GCD) of two numbers is the highest number that divides evenly into both numbers.

The GCD is also known as the greatest common factor (GCF), factor being any number that can evenly divide into a bigger number.

Example:    Find the GCD of 12 and 18.

The normal method is to find the biggest of all factors of each numbers.

The factors of 12 are 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 12.

The factors of 18 are 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18.

The greatest common factor is 6.

Example:    Find the GCD of 42 and 14.

Sometimes the smaller number divides into the big number. In this case, the GCD is 14.
