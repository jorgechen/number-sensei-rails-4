Method

This trick works for repeating decimals like where each decimal has one repeating digit at the end:

  • If the repeating digit is 9:
    1. Ignoring the repeating 9s, add 1 to the non-repeating digits to get a new decimal
    2. This new decimal is the answer. Convert it into a fraction if needed.
  • Otherwise:
    1. For the numerator, join the non-repeating digits with a single repeating digit, and subtract by the non-repeating digits (for .12333, it's 123 - 12)
    2. For the denominator, write 9 and the same amount of 0s as the non-repeating digits
